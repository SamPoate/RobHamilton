The Man

Rob has always had a love for the ocean, travel and endurance events. He is an accomplished scuba diver, having passed his PADI scuba dive instructor course at 18 and going on to complete a number of technical diving qualifications. Rob’s curiosity about the world and how far he can push himself led him to travel to over 40 countries, complete two ultra-mountain marathons and, in 2016, Rob achieved a lifelong ambition and was accepted into the Royal Military Academy Sandhurst. However, soon after he started training, he severely injured his knee and was medically discharged.

While taking part in the Talisker Whisky Atlantic Challenge has been a goal for over 10 years; it is now also a chance for Rob to prove to himself that, despite his medical discharge he is capable of doing incredible things. The Atlantic row also presents a tremendous opportunity to raise money for an organisation which means a lot to Rob. At the start of 2018, Rob spent several months working for the Collateral Repair Project (CRP) in Amman, Jordan. The organisation helps refugees through emergency aid and community programmes such as English classes, after-school clubs and psycho-social support. Rob is hoping to raise at least £10,000 for this amazing organisation.

The Challenge

The Talisker Whisky Atlantic Challenge is often touted as one of the world’s toughest race, and with good reason. The annual race starts ever December in the Canary Islands and finishes 3000 miles later, in Antigua. During the race Rob will have to contend with seasickness, salt sores, sleep deprivation, dehydration, extreme heat, isolation, 20ft high waves, sharks, and possible capsizes. Despite eating over 6000 calories a day, Rob will likely lose at least 12kg by the time he finishes in Antigua. It is perhaps unsurprising that more people have climbed Everest than successfully rowed across an ocean.

While there are two safety yachts for the fleet in case of emergencies, the race itself is unsupported. Rob will carry everything he needs to cross the Atlantic in his boat, this includes enough food for 90 days, a water maker to purify sea water, a satellite phone, life raft, radio and chart plotter. Rob is actively seeking sponsorship to help him achieve this incredible goal. Please download a sponsorship document if you would like more information.

The Charity

Rob is hoping to raise £10,000 for Collateral Repair Project (CRP), a refugee support organisation based in Amman, Jordan. The organisation supports refugees from Syria, Iraq, Somalia and Yemen as well as poorer Jordanians through emergency aid and community programming such as English classes, after-school clubs, trauma relief and women’s empowerment groups. The organisation has special meaning to Rob as he worked there for several months in 2018, helping to manage volunteers who ran crucial programmes.

CRP fosters community and dignity in refugee neighbourhoods, as well as ensuring that basic food and housing needs are met. They provide basic-needs assistance to hundreds of families through in-kind aid, information and referral services, and a monthly food-voucher program that provides eligible households with coupons to purchase fresh produce and groceries. CRP’s Family Resource and Community Centre offers many activities and learning opportunities to allow refugees to begin to rebuild the communities they lost after fleeing their home countries.

CRP’s strengths include a wealth of knowledge about the refugee community in East Amman and strong relationships with the beneficiaries we serve. The community centre is a place where many refugees spend their days. Because of this, CRP has cultivated strong grassroots support from beneficiaries, many of whom go on to become volunteers, helping to assist their neighbours to access programs and services.
